For MP3a, you will support: TheBarrier. This is a Plane with a flattened sphere as its child. The sphere signifies and active region for intersection. Here are the details:

         Plane: Again, my Plane is defined based on a Quad. A plane always displays its current Normal direction. This is shown as a LineSegment with length of 5 located at the center of the Plane.

         TheBarrier: S(12, 12, 1), the flatten sphere is a child with S(1, 1, 0.1)

o    Initial settings: P(0, 2, 13), R(0, 75, 0)

         BarrierControl: Your user has access to the position and rotation of TheBarrier.

         Front: The front of TheBarrier is side where the normal points towards. You can assume no scaling will be applied on TheBarrier plane after your initial settings.

         TravelingBall interaction:

o    A TravelingBall casts shadow (a black flattened sphere) and is displayed on TheBarrier plane when:

  The ball is in front of TheBarrier, AND

  Its projected position is within the active region as signifies by the flattened sphere.

  Indicating line: For clarity, a thin black line segment (width of 0.02) must connect between a TravellingBall and its shadow on the TheBarrier plane.

  NOTE: The shadow must respect the orientation (rotations) of TheBarrier and always stay flat on the plane.

o    A TravelingBall will reflect off TheBarrier if it collides TheBarrier from the front. This is to say, a TravelingBall will pass right through TheBarrier when approaches from behind.

         AimLine interaction:

o    When an AimLine intersects TheBarrier within the active region, the reflection of the line segment that is in front of the TheBarrier should be displayed as a separate green line segment.

o    The length of the green line segment should be exactly the same as the segment of AimLine that is in front of TheBarrier.

o    There is no reflection from the back of TheBarrier.

         Left mouse click:

o    On the floor crates a new instance of TheBarrier, all created barriers have same behavior as above

  Note, with a front- and a back-facing barriers, the traveling ball would bounce between the two

o    On the active region will select and bind a barrier to the BarrierControl

  Make sure you have proper support for two-way binding!

o    On the region outside of active region will select and delete the corresponding barrier

o    MP3 LMB support must be retained:

  Selecting and move LineEndPt

  Left-wall creates AimLine, Back-wall creates BigLine

  Remove lines when over floor

 

Lastly for the BigLine. You know will support interaction with the TravelingBall:

         A TravelingBalls position is projected onto the BigLine (show as a white sphere with radius of 0.5), when

o    Its perpendicular distance is less than 10 AND

o    Its projected position is within the BigLine segment

o    The projected point must be shown at the surface of the BigLine

o    Indicating line: Once again, for clarity, a thin black line segment (width of 0.02) must connect between a TravellingBall and its projection on the BigLine.

o    A TravelingBall reflects off the BigLine if a collision should occur.

         TheBarrier: reflect off when intersecting from the front in the same way as an AimLine
